BioTech Breakthrough Genetics Experiment

What was done in this expirement?

During our BioTech Breakthrough Genetics Experiment, we performed DNA Extraction, PCR Amplification, and, lastly, Gel Electrophoresis. We performed this experiment to compare our DNA to our peers and see if those with the same nationality as us are more likely to match with similar DNA strands. To explore this, we began by using a swab to collect and extract cheek cells, then transferred them with a pipette to the tube containing the buffer solution. After completing this step, we placed the tube in the incubator for 10 minutes at 95 degrees Celsius. This step is breaking down the DNA cells into smaller, easier to compare molecules. This part of the experiment is called DNA Extraction. Next, we removed the tube from the incubator and extracted a small amount of the DNA from the tube into another one along with the primer and master mix. We made sure to flick it around so it's well mixed and then we placed it into the PCR machine. There are a bunch of mixtures and machines during this process. This step is called PCR Amplification. The last and final step is Gel Electrophoresis, a process used to separate molecules and, in our case, DNA, so that we can compare it to our classmates, performed by an electrical force from two magnets on both sides, all while being in a gel substance that's in a box. In this box, there are wells to place a small amount of the DNA solution. This procedure helps us see the bands of DNA more easily so that we can observe the length and similarities between other bands.

Is Race Real Biologically?

The traditional belief about the biological idea of race is that humans from the same race or those who share very similar physical/visible traits have similar DNA. However, modern studies that scientists have conducted, prove this theory to be wrong. Race is not biological, but more of a social construct that has been created. A physical trait that someone has can not tell you what race they are and just because they share that same trait with another being, it does not mean that they share similar DNA to that person. If scientific studies proving that humans already share 99.9 percent of the same DNA and only a small fraction of genes control the phenotypes we possess is not enough evidence to prove that the idea of race being biological is not true, then surely the results in our experiment should. We observed a repeated pattern of people with different races matching more than those who have similar ones, strengthening the argument that race is not biological.
