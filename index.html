"Our Shared Canvas" Screen Printing Project

The Process

Hi, my name is Mariam! Over the course of our “Shared Canvas” Screenprinting Project, I gained digital and hands-on skills, from using software such as Adobe Photoshop and Premiere to measuring, cutting, and meshing wood. Starting from the very beginning of this project, we first used Adobe Photoshop to get a general visual image of the angles and scenes we wanted to include in our documentary that we would later film using the B-Roll we collected over the duration of the project's process. We also used Adobe Photoshop to create the most important part of this project: our screen-printing design. We learned to use different brushes and tools to create a design that visually shows a real-life issue in the world. For me, I focused my design on the genocide that's taking place in Gaza, Palestine. After that, we began to create our dimensional diagrams, cut lists, and color mixing all in Adobe Illustrator. We took a break from all the digital steps of this project and started to measure and cut out our wood based on our cut lists. I personally needed to create 3 frames for all 3 of my colors in my design, so it was a lengthy process. After all pieces of wood were cut, we used wood glue, a stapler, and the help of a partner to assemble the frames. We then took mesh and stretched it across the frames, also with the help of a partner (Thank you to my class for being a great support!). After all the hands on steps were almost done, we transitioned into taking the design we created in the beginning and put each color of the design into a software that would print it onto vinyl. We then took that vinyl, ironed it onto the frame, and peeled away the parts we didn't need. Almost done! Now, the actual screenprinting! We started off with printing on paper to get a feel for the process. It was tideous since each color had to dry in order to print the next color, but eventually we finished. Finally, we took all the B-Roll we had collected over the course of the project and used Adobe Premiere to create a documentary show casing the process it took to make the final screenprinting results. p>
